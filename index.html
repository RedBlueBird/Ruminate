Runic'23 Invitational

A Computer Science stampede between May 19-21st brought to you by MSJHS, AHS, and IHS computer science clubs from the Bay Area. Ranging from Competitive Programming to Web Design, the problems are designed to be beginner-friendly and fun to solve. Enjoy!

How This Works

Strategy & Deception & Puzzles

Everyone will be given programming questions and short responses. Solving a programming problem awards your team points, and solving a short question allow you take points off from other teams. Leading teams at the end of the 3-day stampede will be recognized as winners. It is that simple!

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is this event about?

Runic '23 is programming invitational which will test and push students in computer science clubs around the bay area to their limit. Team will compete together to, in the end, win prizes for themselves and the clubs they are representing. Students will be exposed to concepts from data structures to SQL injections and flatbuffers.

Who is allowed to participate?

Anyone age 13 and up are eligible to compete and earn prizes. If a winning participant is living outside of the US and the physical prizes cannot be shipped to their location, they will be notified ahead of time through email and/or discord.

How many people are allowed on each team?

A team can be either an one-man-army, a three-man-triple, or any number in between!

Do I need to know how to code?

Not necessarily! Coding is only needed when answering programming problems. But for short questions, plain words will do the job just fine!

I am a complete beginner, what do I do?

No worries at all! This competition is designed to be beginner friendly. Here is a list of resources to get you started:
[1] USACO Guide, basic competitive programming algorithms under one curated wiki.
[2] Competitive Programmer's Handbook, an essential study guide and reference book for many applicable programming problems.
[3] ACSL wiki, an introduction to many popular cs topics including boolean algebra and logic gates.
Checkout our discord server for more resources update!

Can people outside the Bay Area participate for fun?

Yes, absolutely! Everyone is welcome to participate as long as they satisify the requirements.

Got more questions?
